Research Overview HCI + AI · 2025 Alandi, Maharashtra

How Rural Marathi Speakers
Experience AI in Their Own Language

An experimental study on language comfort, trust, and engagement with LLM-based assistants among residents of Alandi — a semi-rural community underrepresented in AI research.

Domain Human–Computer Interaction
Population Rural Marathi Speakers, Alandi
Design Within-Subjects Experiment
Sample 20 Participants
The Problem
AI built for the world,
designed for few

LLM-based assistants like ChatGPT are increasingly part of everyday life — but their design assumes English literacy, urban access, and text-comfortable users. In places like Alandi, a semi-rural pilgrimage town near Pune, residents are beginning to encounter AI through smartphones, WhatsApp bots, and government services — yet no research examines how they actually experience it.

Why Alandi?

Our college's location in Alandi makes this community naturally accessible for ethical, grounded research. Alandi represents thousands of similar semi-rural towns where cheap smartphones have arrived, but AI design hasn't caught up — making it an ideal real-world test case.

Research Gap
What no one has studied yet
LLM studies almost exclusively sample English-speaking or urban/professional users.
Indian HCI research targets students or city-based professionals — not rural residents.
No study has experimentally tested how native Marathi comfort shapes AI trust or understanding.
Rural digital literacy as a variable is absent from AI interaction research entirely.
Objectives
What we aim to find out
Compare AI interaction in English vs. Marathi for the same users on the same tasks.
Measure trust, comfort, comprehension, and willingness to reuse AI in each language.
Understand whether task type (casual vs. information-seeking) shifts language preference.
Capture behavioral cues — hesitation, rereading, giving up — that surveys may miss.
Participants
20 Alandi residents, purposively sampled
CriterionProfile
LocationAlandi & surrounding semi-rural area
LanguageDominant Marathi speakers
EducationMix: some schooling to college-level
OccupationFarmers, shopkeepers, pilgrimage workers, students
AI ExposureFirst-time to occasional users (not regular)
Task Design
Grounded in real Alandi needs
Casual Tasks
  • Health advice (fever, stomach ache)
  • Temple timings query
  • Draft a WhatsApp message
Information-Seeking
  • Why crops fail in summer
  • Govt. scheme eligibility (PM Kisan)
  • Understanding bank loan interest

Each participant completes one casual + one information-seeking task in both English and Marathi (order counterbalanced).

Methods
Mixed-methods, within-subjects
Within-Subjects Design Likert Surveys Semi-structured Interviews Behavioral Observation Audio/Video Recording Counterbalanced Order

Observers specifically note: switching to voice input, asking bystanders for help, reading aloud, visible confusion, or task abandonment — behavioral signals often richer than self-report for this population.

Hypotheses
Three testable predictions
H1
Language Comfort: Participants will report higher comfort, trust, and willingness to reuse AI in Marathi than English — especially among those with lower English literacy.
H2
Digital Literacy Moderator: The language comfort advantage will be strongest for participants with lower digital literacy, showing language as a proxy for accessibility.
H3
Task Type: Casual tasks will show a stronger Marathi advantage; scientific tasks may see more tolerance for English regardless of comfort.
Expected Contributions
📖
Academic

First experimental study of native language comfort in rural Indian LLM users. Empirical base for culturally adaptive AI design.

🎨
Design

Recommendations for multilingual AI interfaces. Guidance on task-based language adaptation — when native language matters most.

🌍
Societal

Improves AI accessibility for rural, non-English populations in healthcare, agriculture, and government service contexts.
